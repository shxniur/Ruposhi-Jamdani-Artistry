Our Story

A home for Dhakai Jamdani luxury.

Born in Bangladesh, Ruposhi Jamdani Artistry exists to honor the world-famous Dhakai Jamdani, presenting it as a refined, contemporary luxury while preserving the dignity of the weavers behind every motif.

Each piece is made in limited quantities, designed for those who see Jamdani not just as attire, but as an heirloom that carries memory, culture, and craft across generations.

Signature philosophy

• Slow, intentional production in partnership with master weavers.
• Curated designs that balance tradition with modern silhouettes.
• Transparent storytelling about time, hands, and techniques behind each piece.

Craftsmanship

Woven by master hands in Narayanganj.

Every Ruposhi Jamdani textile is handwoven on traditional looms, often taking 200–400 hours per piece, with motifs counted thread by thread rather than printed or embroidered.

Working directly with weaver families, the brand ensures fair compensation, predictable orders, and recognition of the artisans’ names and stories alongside each design.

Inside the loom

• Traditional pit looms in Jamdani clusters.
• Motifs drafted and improvised by seasoned weavers.
• Quality checks at each stage: yarn, motif, and finish.
• Final inspection and finishing in Dhaka studio.
Ethics & Sustainability

Luxury that honors people and place.

Jamdani is naturally low-impact: handloom weaving, minimal energy usage, and careful sourcing of cotton and dyes. Ruposhi Jamdani Artistry builds on this with transparent pricing, small-batch production, and zero dead stock policy.
